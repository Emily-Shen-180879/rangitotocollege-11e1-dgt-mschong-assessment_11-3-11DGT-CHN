History of Art

Art is an expressive form of creativity, communication, imagination, skill, and human experiance. It is a way for people to express their emotions and experiences in a non-verbal way.

Art can be traced all the way back to the Prehistoric era. Some of the earliest artifacts come from the Paleolithic era, or the Old Stone Age. They often came in the form of rock carvings, engravings, pictorial imagery, sculptures, and arrangements. Prehistoric exsisted from around 40,000-4,000 B.C. During this period, people relied on natural pigments and stone carvings to create artworks.

As time progressed, so did art, from 4,000 B.C-A.D. 400 Anicient art was produced by advanced civilizations such as Mesopotamia, Greece, Egypt, and those of the Americas.

This evolved to Medieval art, which spanned from 500-1400. Art continued to evolve until it reached Contemporary Art (1970-present).
